As the economy is being impacted by the pandemic outbreak, people need a way to build their work experience to compete for the limited opportunities in the current job market. Our team has come up with an app for students/new grads to create their own/join projects, find teammates, and connect with recruiters.

Research


(Click & Swipe to see more details)

Competitor analysis


From the competitive analysis, we realized that there are a lot of job/internship posting platforms; however, there is a limited number of apps that provides opportunities for users to build hand-on work experience and enrich career related skills.

For those online courses, programs and/or forums that help people gain hand-on experience by working together in groups, they are not really effective at helping people to connect and lack important features that support group-work such as collaborative workspace.

Hence we want to create an app that dedicates to helping users to connect with others to work on hand-on projects.

  Scope   



Through our research, we were able to refine our problem statement:




How might we design a platform in order to help college students improve their chances of getting a future job during and after the pandemic?

Constraints & Assumptions

We hoped to learn from recruiters and their experience with the hiring process during the pandemic to understand the problems from both perspectives. However, due to the constraint of time and resource, we were not able to interview any real recruiters nor we could get into contact with the UCSD career center. Instead, we relied on online resources and our assumptions based upon past interactions to create a fictional persona for the recruiters of the company.

Storyboard


Based on the design opportunities, we ideated several “how might we...” questions and each came up with a storyboard. We then conducted 5 interviews to understand:


  • What do users like/dislike about each storyboard
  • Do they understand each section(context, problem, solution, resolution) of each storyboard
  • Do they see any issues for the solution and What can be improved
  • And overall, which storyboard they are most related to and which solution they would personally use or recommend to a friend

Iteration

After finishing the high-fidelity prototype, we performed user testing on 5 interviewees by asking them:

  • Demorgraphic questions including age, current job status, familiarity with other job searching Apps like Linkedin etc.
  • To perform a series of tasks: sign in, create a project, search for teammate to join project, check who else is interested in joining the project user has created, find a specific project and apply for a specfic role, edit their own profiles, turn on notification, log out.
  • Overall questions concerning their experience with the app, what do they like the most/ least about the product. What surprised them about the experience, what caused their frustration and how likely would they recommend this to a friend




Overall, all interviewees thought that the experience of the app was great. On a scale from 1 to 5, interviewees would likely recommend the app to a friend with an average score of 4.3.


                    




Nevertheless, there were some improvements for better usability:


Unclear signifier:

  • 3 out of 5 interviewees didn’t know where to search for people

Interrupted user flow(not intuitive/not visible enough):

  • Users felt lost after clicking creating the project because the page directed was the main page of Dashboard instead of the project description page
  • 2 users went to projects page on Dashboard to see who is interested in the project (the questions asked was “What would you do if you wanted to check who is interested in your project?”: the intened route was user click Notification to check)
  • Users reported that they wanted to know who is the project owner on Dshboard for better project management and for distinguishing projects created by themselves or others



Others:

Other suggestion that interviewees have mentioned:

  • Send a quick recruiting message for quick and mass recruiting
  • Adding the user’s response rate to messages would be helpful for the recruiter with the recruiting process
  • More details description of the skills and tools required for the specific roles.

After our discussion, we as a team decided not to consider adding those functionalities for now as we are trying to finish the MVP of the app. We do think that those functions are add-ons that would improve the usability of the app.

Final product

Splash Page


The splash page quickly explains the app to a new user.

Upon logging in, you can create a new project and see recommendations for projects that you might be interested in on the home screen.







Create new project


By clicking "create new project", you can customize project name, description, project roles and deadlines.

Upon hitting “Create” button, it will direct to project details inside Dashboard which displays all the information. On this page, you can add different links and files, such as creating a team slack link and figma link, or adding project code files into your file forlders.

Dashboard


Dashboard is a place where you can see everything that has to do wiht you the user.

User can see your current and past projects, along with editing your profile and changing your settings.










Profile


From the dashboard, user can access their profile by clicking on the profile picture at the top right corner. In there, they can update their skill set and the experience that they have.

The menu also lead to the Account setting to change the profile privacy, Frequently questions section to learn more about the app, and log-out panel to log-out.

Recruit teammate


When needing more help, user can recruit people by adding more roles into the projects through the recruit button in the project main page.

After adding a role, user can track interested users who are interested in the added role by clicking on the icon.











Apply for a project


With our application page, you can find different projects that other users have developed. You can view the different roles that the project requires, such as content strategists and UX designers.

You will also view the other users who already a part of the project to get a better feel for whether or not the project is up your ally. If you feels like the project is a good match, you can pick your desired role and apply for it, which will directly notify the creator about your interest!

Notification


In the Notification, you can see who have accepted your requests to join a project or incoming requests from other users.




Recruiter picked


Users can also conveniently find on our home page a set list of project prompts that recruiters from top companies have provide on our platform.

They will be able to develop projects following these prompts, which will simplify the team matching experience in remote large-scale company-sponsored project building events, such as hackathons and design sprints.

Takeaways


Find the right solution for the root cause

Along the way, our project has received feedback on how related the concepts are to the current situation and they will definitely use the app like this to connect with others and start their own projects. It makes me realize that it's pivotal to anchor the most urgent problem in the first place for the following design to work. For instance, in the survey response, we also got many responses concerning respondants' emotional states and it turned out that many groups had explored the idea of relaxing and alleviating mental stress. But we studied deeper and figured the root causes that people were anxious and unmotivated because of the cancellation of their internships, the layoffs/ freezing hiring process of companies, and that they feel unqualified due to lack of connections and experience. With those root causes in mind, we divergently thought of solutions from different stages including improving skills, getting connections, and applying for jobs. Taking advantage of spotting the right problem and ideating various ideas, our project stands out.
